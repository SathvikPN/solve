Preparing for an SDE-2 role at Amazon within a two-week timeframe is ambitious but achievable with a structured plan. Based on insights from previous interview experiences , here's a focused roadmap to guide your preparation:

**Week 1: Core Technical Skills**

**Day 1-2: Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)**

- **Topics to Cover:**
  - Arrays and Strings
  - Linked Lists
  - Stacks and Queues
  - Trees and Graphs
  - Hash Tables

- **Action Plan:**
  - Review fundamental concepts and common operations for each data structure.
  - Solve 2-3 problems daily on platforms like LeetCode or GeeksforGeeks.

**Day 3-4: Advanced Algorithms**

- **Topics to Cover:**
  - Dynamic Programming
  - Greedy Algorithms
  - Backtracking
  - Sorting and Searching Algorithms

- **Action Plan:**
  - Study algorithmic paradigms and their applications.
  - Practice problems focusing on each algorithm type.

**Day 5-6: System Design Fundamentals**

- **Topics to Cover:**
  - High-Level Design Principles
  - Scalability and Load Balancing
  - Caching Strategies
  - Database Design and Sharding

- **Action Plan:**
  - Read materials on system design basics.
  - Analyze case studies of scalable systems.

**Day 7: Behavioral Preparation**

- **Topics to Cover:**
  - Amazon's Leadership Principles
  - STAR Method for Behavioral Questions

- **Action Plan:**
  - Reflect on past experiences that align with each leadership principle.
  - Prepare concise stories using the STAR method.

**Week 2: Applied Practice and Mock Interviews**

**Day 8-9: Coding Practice**

- **Action Plan:**
  - Engage in timed coding sessions to simulate interview conditions.
  - Focus on medium to hard problems, especially those involving dynamic programming and trees.

**Day 10: System Design Deep Dive**

- **Action Plan:**
  - Work on designing a complex system (e.g., URL shortening service, messaging queue).
  - Discuss your design with a peer or mentor for feedback.

**Day 11: Low-Level Design (LLD)**

- **Topics to Cover:**
  - Object-Oriented Design Principles
  - Design Patterns

- **Action Plan:**
  - Design classes for a given problem statement (e.g., Library Management System).
  - Ensure adherence to SOLID principles.

**Day 12: Mock Interviews**

- **Action Plan:**
  - Schedule mock interviews focusing on both technical and behavioral aspects.
  - Seek feedback to identify areas for improvement.

**Day 13: Review and Reflect**

- **Action Plan:**
  - Revisit challenging topics or problems encountered during the week.
  - Refine behavioral stories and ensure clarity and relevance.

**Day 14: Rest and Final Preparation**

- **Action Plan:**
  - Take a break to relax and recharge.
  - Prepare logistics for the interview day (e.g., setting up a quiet environment, ensuring a stable internet connection).

**Additional Tips:**

- **Consistency:** Dedicate 4-5 focused hours daily as planned.
- **Resources:** Utilize reputable platforms like LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks, and system design interview books.
- **Feedback:** Engage with peers or mentors to gain insights and constructive criticism.
- **Health:** Maintain a balanced routine with adequate rest and nutrition to keep your mind sharp.

By following this roadmap, you'll systematically cover the essential areas required for the SDE-2 interview at Amazon. Stay disciplined, seek help when needed, and approach each day with a growth mindset. Best of luck with your preparation!
